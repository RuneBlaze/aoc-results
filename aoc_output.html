Column names:

  • variant: the current "subset" of the clustering that we are looking at. Note that the subset is done considering the original clustering. That is, tree clusters might be augmented to be non-tree clusters, but they are still labeled as only_tree (with augmented = true)
    • all: no clusters thrown out
    • size_ge_11: cluster size >= 11
    • not_tree: those that are not trees
    • not_tree+size_ge_11: the combination of above two
    • only_tree: those that are trees
  • augmented: true if it is the post-AOC clustering, false if it is the pre-AOC clustering
  • # clusters: # of non-singleton clusters
  • # singletons: # of singleton clusters
  • % augmented: among all non-singleton clusters, percentage of clusters that got augmented
  • relative increase in size, distribution, among ALL non-singleton clusters of (new_size - old_size) / old_size
  • Node Coverage: percentage of nodes that fall in non-singleton clusters
  • Edge Coverage: percentage of edges u, v s.t. $\exists c: u, v \in K_c$
  • Modularity: vanilla modularity distribution of the clusters
  • MCD: distribution of MCD of clusters
  • Clusters Per Node: distribution of number of clusters assigned to each node, including singletons
  • CPM: distribution of CPM of clusters, with the correct resolution value of non-singletons
  • Size: distribution of cluster size of non-singletons
  • Deviation from Treeness: distribution of $(m - n + 1)/n$ of non-singletons
  • Density: distribution $2 m / n (n - 1)$ of non-singletons
